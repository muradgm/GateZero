# GateZero Offer

## Purpose

This document defines the honest current offer for GateZero.

It keeps the product from drifting into bot hype, trading guru language, or public SaaS claims
before the system earns that right.

## Best Current Offer

> Give GateZero a strategy idea and a market dataset, and it will help you test the strategy, expose
> the risks, review the evidence, and decide whether it deserves paper trading.

## What We Are Selling Internally

At the current stage, GateZero sells discipline to the operator.

It helps answer:

- Is this strategy idea even testable?
- Is the backtest honest?
- What assumptions could be fooling me?
- What is the drawdown profile?
- What happens after fees and slippage?
- Is the strategy stable across periods?
- Should this stay research-only, move to paper trading, or be rejected?

## What We Are Not Selling

GateZero does not sell:

- guaranteed profit
- AI buy/sell calls
- live trading autonomy
- leverage amplification
- social-media signal chasing
- market prediction certainty
- hands-free income

## Target User

Best fit now:

- one serious retail operator
- one founder/builder learning trading systems
- one disciplined technical user who wants research, risk review, and process control

Not best fit now:

- beginners looking for passive income
- users wanting guaranteed signals
- high-frequency traders
- multi-user trading desks
- public investment clients

## Current Demo Loop

The strongest honest demo loop is:

```text
Strategy idea -> historical data -> backtest run -> metric report -> risk review -> pass/fail maturity verdict
```

Later demo loops may add:

```text
paper trade -> operator review -> mistake ledger -> learning update
```

and only much later:

```text
supervised live trade -> execution audit -> risk outcome -> learning event
```

## Offer Guardrails

Any product copy, demo script, or agent output must stay inside these rules:

1. Say “research” before “trade.”
2. Say “risk approval” before “execution.”
3. Say “paper trading” before “live trading.”
4. Say “operator decision” before “automation.”
5. Explain uncertainty clearly.
6. Make blocked decisions feel like successful safety outcomes.
