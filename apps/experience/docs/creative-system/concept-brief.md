# Concept Brief — Conviction Atlas

## Product

TraderFrame is a private trading decision-intelligence platform that interprets market structure, events, sentiment, flows, technical evidence, and risk context to form an explainable directional thesis.

## Wedge

> TraderFrame turns fragmented market evidence into one explainable, risk-aware trading decision.

## User outcome

The user should understand:

- what is moving the market,
- which outcomes are plausible,
- what evidence supports or contradicts each outcome,
- how risk changes the decision,
- and whether to act, wait, reduce, or reject.

## Core metaphor

TraderFrame constructs a changing topographic map of possible market futures.

Evidence alters the terrain. Strong evidence raises and connects viable routes. Contradiction splits paths. Risk creates unstable or prohibited regions. The platform highlights the best current route while keeping alternatives and invalidation visible.

## Narrative flow

```text
01 Ingest
Coherent streams of market evidence enter as contour-aligned flows.

02 Map
The system constructs a neutral market terrain from context and relationships.

03 Weight
Evidence changes elevation, path strength, and probability.

04 Filter Risk
Unsafe routes collapse, fade, or become restricted regions.

05 Choose
One route becomes the highest-quality current thesis.

06 Decide
TraderFrame presents a conditional recommendation, confidence, risk, timeframe, and invalidation.
```

## Important refinement

Raw data must not appear as unrelated floating cubes or generic particles. It should enter as structured strands, contour fragments, vector fields, or layered signals that naturally become the terrain and routes.

## Visual principles

- Dark, spatial, editorial environment
- Fine topographic contour system
- Evidence represented as coherent flowing lines
- Probability expressed through path strength, elevation, clarity, and persistence
- Risk represented through terrain instability, obstruction, heat, or route collapse
- Recommended route is clear but never presented as certainty
- UI behaves like an analytical overlay, not a sci-fi dashboard

## Motion principles

- Ingest flows with directional intent
- Mapping grows from relationships, not from random reveal
- Weighting changes the whole field gradually
- Contradiction produces branching and tension
- Risk slows, interrupts, or collapses paths
- Recommendation resolves calmly
- No constant ambient movement without semantic purpose

## Colour semantics

- Cyan: current verified evidence
- Violet: selected thesis and recommendation
- Amber: uncertainty, counter-thesis, or review
- Red: invalidation or prohibited risk only
- Neutral silver: context, baseline, and inactive paths

## Product-language constraints

Use:

- directional thesis
- estimated probability
- confidence
- supporting evidence
- contradictory evidence
- risk
- invalidation
- recommendation

Avoid:

- guaranteed prediction
- certainty
- winning signal
- autonomous execution
- market-beating claims

## Success criteria

The concept succeeds when a viewer can explain the product without reading a long description:

> TraderFrame maps possible market outcomes, weighs the evidence, removes weak or unsafe routes, and recommends the strongest rational path while showing risk and uncertainty.
