# Concept Brief — Conviction Atlas

## Product

TraderFrame is a private trading decision-intelligence platform that interprets market structure, events, sentiment, flows, technical evidence, and risk context to form an explainable directional thesis.

## Wedge

> TraderFrame turns fragmented market evidence into one explainable, risk-aware trading decision.

## User outcome

The user should understand:

- what is moving the market,
- which outcomes are plausible,
- what evidence supports or contradicts each outcome,
- how risk changes the decision,
- and whether to act, wait, reduce, or reject.

## Core metaphor

TraderFrame creates a living topographic field of possible market futures.

Incoming intelligence appears as coherent threads carrying origin, confidence, age, importance, and category. These threads carve and reshape the terrain. Agreement strengthens routes. Contradiction creates fractures and competing branches. Risk produces valleys, unstable regions, heat, or collapse. Conviction is not announced; it grows until one conditional path becomes the strongest current thesis.

The experience must feel like witnessing the market think, not watching an infographic explain it.

## Narrative flow

```text
01 Observe
Signals arrive as living intelligence threads. They remain fragmented and unresolved.

02 Correlate
Relationships form. Agreeing threads merge, conflicting threads split, and expired evidence fades.

03 Form Thesis
The market field rises and changes. Several possible futures become visible as competing routes.

04 Challenge
Counter-evidence attacks the thesis. Risk reshapes the terrain and weak routes fracture, sink, or disappear.

05 Conviction
One route becomes the strongest current probability. Alternatives and invalidation remain visible.

06 Execute
The landscape folds and flattens into TraderFrame. The product interface emerges from the same geometry without a cut or overlay.
```

## Visual operating system

### Intelligence language

Represent meaning, not generic data.

- Use living threads, layered signals, contour fragments, and vector flows.
- Agreement causes merging.
- Conflict causes branching and tension.
- Expiry causes fading and loss of influence.
- Importance changes thickness, brightness, and persistence.

Do not use unrelated cubes, dots, floating icons, or decorative particles as the primary intelligence metaphor.

### Geometry language

Everything should feel generated by invisible forces.

Use:

- topographic contours,
- magnetic or vector fields,
- fluid lines,
- geological folds,
- fractures,
- pressure and elevation.

Avoid mechanical gates, rotating machinery, and hard sci-fi architecture.

### Material language

Preferred materials:

- smoked glass,
- obsidian,
- polished graphite,
- optical acrylic,
- liquid light.

Chrome and exposed metal should be rare and functional, never the main visual identity.

### Motion language

Motion communicates reasoning.

```text
accelerate
→ branch
→ collide
→ slow
→ stabilize
→ resolve
```

No object should rotate, pulse, or float only because it looks attractive.

### Landscape language

- Probability raises terrain and strengthens routes.
- Confidence increases clarity, elevation, and persistence.
- Risk creates valleys, unstable regions, heat, and collapse.
- Contradiction fractures the field and produces competing paths.
- Liquidity creates continuity and flow.

The terrain is the intelligence model, not a decorative background.

### Decision language

Show many possible paths first. The selected route should emerge gradually, almost like roots growing beneath the terrain. Weak routes recede rather than disappearing instantly.

The final path communicates highest current probability, never certainty.

### Interface language

The UI must grow out of the landscape:

```text
contours flatten
→ surfaces align
→ panels form
→ cards resolve
→ TraderFrame emerges
```

Do not place a conventional dashboard on top of the scene.

## Hero sequence

1. A camera observes an almost-black infinite field from high above.
2. Living intelligence threads enter from different directions.
3. Threads carve contours and raise the market terrain.
4. Multiple future routes emerge.
5. Counter-evidence fractures several routes.
6. Risk reshapes the field and removes weak options.
7. One conditional path grows brighter and more stable.
8. The camera dives toward it.
9. The landscape folds upward and becomes the TraderFrame workspace.

## Colour semantics

- Cyan: verified evidence and current intelligence flow.
- Violet: selected thesis, conviction, and recommendation.
- Amber: uncertainty, counter-thesis, and review.
- Red: invalidation or prohibited risk only.
- Neutral silver: context, baseline terrain, and inactive paths.

## Design principles

1. Intelligence should flow, not float.
2. Probability should emerge, not appear.
3. Risk should reshape, not merely block.
4. Conviction should grow, not flash.
5. The interface should evolve, not overlay.
6. Motion must always communicate reasoning.
7. Every visual element must answer: "What does this tell the trader about the quality of this decision?"
8. Probability, never certainty.
9. The user retains final control.
10. Private by design.

## Product-language constraints

Use:

- directional thesis,
- estimated probability,
- confidence,
- supporting evidence,
- contradictory evidence,
- risk,
- invalidation,
- conditional recommendation.

Avoid:

- guaranteed prediction,
- certainty,
- winning signal,
- autonomous execution,
- market-beating claims.

## Success criteria

The concept succeeds when a viewer can explain the product without reading a long description:

> TraderFrame watches market intelligence form relationships, maps several possible futures, challenges them with contradiction and risk, and lets the strongest explainable path emerge into a user-controlled decision.
