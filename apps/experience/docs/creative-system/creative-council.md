# TraderFrame Creative Council

The council reviews every major concept and production gate.

## Permanent roles

### Product Strategy Director
Keeps the work aligned to the user problem, category, wedge, and action model.

### Creative Director
Owns the big idea, memorability, originality, and conceptual coherence.

### Senior Art Director
Owns composition, visual hierarchy, materials, typography, lighting, and craft.

### Information Designer
Ensures evidence, probability, contradiction, risk, and recommendation are understandable.

### Motion Director
Defines motion as product meaning: formation, weighting, branching, obstruction, resolution, and change.

### Experience Designer
Owns user control, interaction, pacing, responsive behaviour, and accessibility.

### Trading Domain Specialist
Challenges misleading assumptions about prediction, confidence, risk, market direction, timing, and execution.

### Trust and Governance Reviewer
Prevents certainty theatre, unsupported performance claims, hidden contradictions, or autonomous-decision implications.

### Creative Technologist
Tests feasibility, architecture, browser constraints, asset budgets, and runtime performance.

## Decision rules

- Product truth outranks spectacle.
- A concept fails if it could advertise an unrelated AI or fintech product.
- Every recommendation state must expose uncertainty and invalidation.
- Risk is allowed to override directional confidence.
- The user remains the final decision-maker.
- Motion must explain a state transition.
- Mobile and reduced-motion modes are first-class outputs.
- Approval requires agreement from Product Strategy, Trading Domain, Trust, and Creative Technology.

## Review format

Each review records:

1. Decision requested
2. Evidence reviewed
3. Strongest argument for approval
4. Strongest argument against approval
5. Risks and unresolved questions
6. Decision
7. Required changes
8. Owner and deadline
