# Layer 1 — Creative Thinking

This layer establishes meaning before execution.

## 1. Product Strategy Director

### Owns

- Product definition
- User problem
- Product wedge
- Category and positioning
- Action model
- Success criteria

### Required outputs

- Product definition
- Target user and context
- Wedge statement
- Product promise
- Differentiation statement
- Approved vocabulary and prohibited claims

### Approval question

> Does the concept explain TraderFrame’s unique value rather than merely looking like financial technology?

---

## 2. Creative Director

### Owns

- Central idea
- Memorability logic
- Emotional direction
- Experience principles
- Selection of creative territory

### Required outputs

- Three distinct creative territories
- One approved north-star concept
- Experience manifesto
- Visual and interaction principles

### Approval question

> Why will someone remember this experience after the visual spectacle is gone?

---

## 3. Information Architect

### Owns

How intelligence becomes visible and understandable.

### Core model

```text
Evidence
→ Context
→ Relationships
→ Probability
→ Contradiction
→ Risk
→ Conviction
→ Recommendation
```

### Required outputs

- Information-state model
- Evidence taxonomy
- Probability and confidence model
- Risk and contradiction states
- Mapping from data states to visual behaviour

### Approval question

> Can the user understand what changed, why the recommendation changed, and what evidence opposes it?

---

## 4. Story Experience Director

### Owns

- Narrative arc
- Pacing
- Tension and release
- Interaction moments
- Camera intent
- Transition logic

### Required outputs

- Experience beat sheet
- User journey
- Scene sequence
- Interaction map
- Emotional curve

### Approval question

> Does each moment advance the meaning of the product rather than decorate it?

---

## 5. Senior Art Director

### Owns

- Composition
- Material language
- Lighting
- Typography
- Colour semantics
- Image and UI hierarchy

### Required outputs

- Art-direction board
- Material and lighting bible
- Colour semantics
- Type and layout principles
- Visual acceptance criteria

### Approval question

> Is the work visually ownable, coherent, legible, and feasible on the web?

---

## Layer 1 approval gate

Production begins only when all of the following are true:

- The wedge is visible in the concept.
- Probability is not represented as certainty.
- Risk can change or block the recommendation.
- Contradictory evidence remains visible.
- The user retains final control.
- The concept has a distinctive visual behaviour, not only a distinctive object.
- The concept can be expressed on desktop, mobile, and reduced-motion modes.
