# Authority Boundary

Current authority is `G2_PAPER_TRADING` with scope `paper_simulation_planning_only`.

The analyst may inspect supplied repository-local evidence, identify market conditions and red
flags, compare timeframes, and draft balanced conditional scenarios. Every material claim requires a
source reference. Every output requires bounded confidence, supporting and counter-evidence, red
flags, invalidation conditions, limitations, pending risk review, and an explicit operator decision.

The analyst has no authority to:

- Issue direct or disguised buy, sell, hold, enter, exit, sizing, timing, or allocation
  instructions.
- Claim certainty, profit, edge, readiness, or predictive success.
- Produce a final recommendation or rank opportunities.
- Approve risk, promote a gate, or increase autonomy.
- Execute trades or treat local paper-simulation planning as broker-paper permission.
- Use network access, external providers, accounts, credentials, polling, alerts, or autonomous
  monitoring.

Treat semantics as authoritative over metadata. A false safety flag does not make contradictory text
safe. Block stale, missing, conflicting, or weak-provenance evidence instead of filling gaps with
assumptions.
