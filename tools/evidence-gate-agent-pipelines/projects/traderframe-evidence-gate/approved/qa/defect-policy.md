To define the release severity levels for TraderFrame's Evidence Gate, we need to consider the potential impact of defects on the user experience and business goals. Here are the proposed severity levels:

### Severity Levels

1. **Critical (Level 1)**
   - **Definition:** Defects that prevent users from accessing core functionality or cause significant data loss.
   - **Examples:**
     - Complete loss of user session or data.
     - Inability to log in or access the trading interface.
     - Failure to load critical components of the interactive 3D narrative.

2. **High (Level 2)**
   - **Definition:** Defects that severely impact usability, functionality, or business processes but do not prevent core operations.
   - **Examples:**
     - Partial loss of user session.
     - Inability to perform critical actions like placing a trade.
     - Significant delays in the interactive 3D narrative.

3. **Medium (Level 3)**
   - **Definition:** Defects that cause minor usability issues, functionality limitations, or business process disruptions but do not significantly impact core operations.
   - **Examples:**
     - Minor interface glitches.
     - Inability to filter evidence in the decision-making process.
     - Slight delays in loading less critical components of the interactive 3D narrative.

4. **Low (Level 4)**
   - **Definition:** Defects that are minor usability issues, functionality limitations, or business process disruptions with minimal impact on core operations.
   - **Examples:**
     - Minor interface inconsistencies.
     - Slight delays in less critical parts of the interactive 3D narrative.
     - Minor issues with data recording and transparency.

### Guidelines for Prioritization

1. **Impact on User Experience:** Assess how the defect affects the user's ability to perform their tasks effectively.
2. **Business Impact:** Evaluate the impact on the business goals, such as maintaining market conviction or ensuring transparent reasoning.
3. **Severity of Data Loss:** Consider whether the defect could result in data loss or corruption.
4. **Impact on User Control and Risk Awareness:** Assess how the defect affects the user's ability to control their decisions and understand risks.

### Example Scenarios

1. **Critical (Level 1):**
   - A critical bug causes the interactive 3D narrative to crash, preventing users from accessing any trading features.
   - A major interface glitch prevents users from logging in or placing trades.

2. **High (Level 2):**
   - A partial loss of user session that requires manual re-login.
   - Significant delays in loading critical components of the interactive 3D narrative, causing frustration.

3. **Medium (Level 3):**
   - Minor interface glitches that do not affect core functionality.
   - Inability to filter evidence in the decision-making process for a short period.

4. **Low (Level 4):**
   - Minor issues with data recording and transparency that do not impact critical operations.
   - Slight delays in loading less critical parts of the interactive 3D narrative.

By defining these severity levels, we can prioritize defects effectively, ensuring that the most impactful issues are addressed first while still allowing for incremental improvements.
