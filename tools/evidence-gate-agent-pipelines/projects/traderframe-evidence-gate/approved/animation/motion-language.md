**Motion Principle Extraction for TraderFrame — Evidence Gate**

---

### **Core Narrative Summary**
The *Evidence Gate* is the central decision-making checkpoint in TraderFrame. It represents the moment when traders and portfolio managers evaluate, challenge, and approve market evidence before it becomes a credible, risk-aware decision. The narrative is about **transparency, control, and precision** in the face of complex market data.

---

## **Motion Principles**

### **1. Evidence Filtering (Gate Entry)**
- **Motion Rule**: Slow, deliberate motion as evidence is filtered through layers of data.
- **Camera**: Low-angle tracking shot, moving upward as the gate opens, revealing the evidence stream.
- **Visuals**: Data streams appear as translucent, glowing lines, with filters as translucent panels that move in and out.
- **Sound**: Subtle, rhythmic pulses to indicate data flow and processing.

### **2. Challenge (Gate Review)**
- **Motion Rule**: Dynamic, tense motion as the evidence is challenged by the user.
- **Camera**: Over-the-shoulder shot, following the user’s interaction with the interface.
- **Visuals**: Evidence elements flicker, pulse, or distort when challenged; user interface elements highlight with color shifts.
- **Sound**: Sharp, rhythmic clicks or tones to indicate challenge confirmation.

### **3. Approval (Gate Passage)**
- **Motion Rule**: Smooth, confident motion as the evidence is approved and recorded.
- **Camera**: Tracking shot from the user’s perspective, moving forward as the gate opens.
- **Visuals**: Evidence elements solidify, color shifts to a trusted green, and a confirmation icon appears.
- **Sound**: Clear, satisfying "click" or "ding" with a slight delay to emphasize the decision.

### **4. Recording (Gate Exit)**
- **Motion Rule**: Calm, steady motion as the decision is recorded and becomes part of the decision history.
- **Camera**: Wide-angle shot, showing the user and the system interface, with a slow zoom out to show the decision being logged.
- **Visuals**: A timeline or ledger appears, with the decision marked as "recorded" with a timestamp.
- **Sound**: Gentle, ambient tone to indicate the completion of the process.

---

## **Camera Choreography**

| Scene | Camera Type | Movement | Focus | Purpose |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------|---------|
| **Filtering** | Low-angle tracking | Upward | Evidence stream | Establishes the gate as a threshold |
| **Challenge** | Over-the-shoulder | Follows user | Interface elements | Shows user control and tension |
| **Approval** | Tracking | Forward | User and interface | Confirms decision with confidence |
| **Recording** | Wide-angle | Zoom out | Timeline/ledger | Shows the decision’s permanence |

---

## **Visual Motifs**

- **Gate**: A translucent, glowing structure that opens and closes.
- **Evidence Stream**: Glowing, flowing lines that solidify upon approval.
- **Interface Elements**: Color-shifts and pulsations to indicate status.
- **Timeline/Ledger**: A subtle, floating panel that logs decisions.

---

## **Sound Design Notes**

- **Filtering**: Subtle, rhythmic pulses.
- **Challenge**: Sharp, rhythmic clicks or tones.
- **Approval**: Clear, satisfying "click" or "ding."
- **Recording**: Gentle ambient tone to indicate completion.

---

## **Brand Alignment**

- **Evidence First**: Visuals and motion emphasize data flow and filtering.
- **Transparent Reasoning**: Color shifts and interface feedback show the decision process.
- **User Control**: Camera follows user, and interface responds to their actions.
- **Risk Awareness**: Tension in motion and sound underscores the gravity of the decision.
- **Calm Precision**: Smooth, deliberate motion and sound design reflect the brand’s tone.

---

## **Implementation Notes**

- All motion should be **smooth, deliberate, and scalable** for different screen sizes.
- Use **color as a visual cue** for status (e.g., red = challenge, green = approval).
- **Camera transitions** should be **subtle and intuitive**, avoiding abrupt cuts.
- **Sound should be layered** to support the narrative without overwhelming the user.
