---

### **1. Lens & Framing Philosophy**  
**Lens:**  
- **Cinematic Depth Lens (Cinematic 3D)** — A hybrid of **cinematic depth** and **interactive UI depth** to reflect the layered nature of market evidence.  
- **Dynamic Focal Shifts** — Use of **focus pull** and **depth of field** to guide attention between data layers, user controls, and visual metaphors (e.g., "evidence gates").  
- **High Dynamic Range (HDR)** — To emphasize contrast between **risk zones** (dark, shadowed) and **evidence clarity** (bright, sharp).  

**Framing Philosophy:**  
- **Asymmetrical Framing** — To reflect the **asymmetry of risk and evidence**.  
- **Framing as a Metaphor** — Use of **gates, doors, and thresholds** to visually represent the **evidence gate process**.  
- **User-Centric Framing** — The user (trader) is always **centered or in focus**, with **environmental context** (market data, risk indicators) **surrounding them**.  

---

### **2. Movement & Transitions**  
**Movement Style:**  
- **Calm Precision** — Movement is **smooth, deliberate, and rhythmic**, mirroring the **calm precision** of the brand.  
- **Dynamic Transitions** — Use of **fluid transitions** between **evidence layers**, **risk zones**, and **decision nodes**.  
- **Camera as a Thought Process** — The camera **moves with the user’s thought process** — from **exploration** to **analysis** to **decision**.  

**Transition Types:**  
- **Fade Through Evidence** — A **slow fade** through layers of data, symbolizing the **filtering of evidence**.  
- **Gate Slides** — A **horizontal slide** revealing a new **evidence gate** or **risk indicator**.  
- **Zoom Through Time** — A **zoom-in** on a **historical data point** to show **evidence validation** over time.  
- **Camera Pull Back** — To emphasize **risk awareness** and **contextual clarity**.  

---

### **3. Depth & Layering**  
**Depth Strategy:**  
- **3D Layering** — Use of **depth layers** to represent **evidence**, **risk**, and **decision**.  
- **Depth as Trust** — **Shallow depth** for **uncertain evidence**, **medium depth** for **validated data**, and **deep focus** for **approved decisions**.  
- **Depth as Time** — Use of **depth of field** to show **evidence aging** or **risk evolving** over time.  

**Layering Techniques:**  
- **Depth of Field (DoF)** — To guide the viewer’s attention between **evidence layers** and **risk indicators**.  
- **3D UI Elements** — Use of **3D UI elements** (e.g., floating evidence cards, risk indicators) to create **interactive depth**.  
- **Depth as a Metaphor** — The **depth of the scene** reflects the **depth of analysis** and **risk awareness**.
