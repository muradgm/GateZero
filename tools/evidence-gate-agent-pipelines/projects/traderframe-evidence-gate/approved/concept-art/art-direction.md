**TraderFrame — Evidence Gate**  
**Art Direction Specification**  
**Stage: Art Direction Specification**  
**Objective: Define composition, camera, lighting, materials, color and atmosphere**  

---

### **1. Composition & Layout**

**Core Visual Metaphor:**  
The *Evidence Gate* is a **portal** — a physical and conceptual threshold between raw market data and the final, credible decision. It is a **monolithic structure**, both **geometric and organic**, symbolizing the intersection of logic and intuition in trading.

**Layout:**  
- **Central Axis:** A vertical **gate-like structure** (simplified as a **tall, translucent column**) forms the visual anchor.  
- **Surrounding Elements:** 
  - **Data Streams** (digital ribbons or lines) flow into the gate, representing incoming market evidence.  
  - **Decision Nodes** (small, glowing orbs or icons) emerge from the gate, symbolizing approved, validated decisions.  
  - **User Interface Elements** (e.g., sliders, graphs, text overlays) are subtly integrated into the environment, reinforcing the idea of a **decision-making interface**.  
- **Perspective:** A **mid-ground perspective** with **depth** to emphasize the gate as a **threshold** — the user is *looking through* it, not at it.

---

### **2. Camera & Motion**

**Camera Style:**  
- **Narrative Camera:** A **slow, deliberate motion** that guides the viewer through the gate, mimicking the process of **filtering, challenging, approving, and recording** evidence.  
- **Camera Path:** A **diagonal sweep** from the **lower left to upper right**, passing through the gate, emphasizing **movement and transformation**.  
- **Focus Points:** The **gate itself** is the primary focus, with **secondary focus** on the **data streams** and **decision nodes** as they interact with the gate.

**Camera Angles:**  
- **Wide shots** to emphasize the scale of the gate and its integration into the environment.  
- **Close-ups** on the gate’s surface to show **transparency, texture, and interaction** with data.  
- **Over-the-shoulder** shots to simulate a user interface or a trader’s perspective.

---

### **3. Lighting & Atmosphere**

**Lighting Style:**  
- **Calm, precise lighting** that reflects the **brand principles** of **calm precision** and **risk awareness**.  
- **Natural light** with **subtle digital glow** to emphasize the **intersection of physical and digital**.  
- **Soft ambient lighting** to create a **professional, serene atmosphere**.

**Light Sources:**  
- **Backlighting** on the gate to create a **translucent, glowing effect**.  
- **Soft directional light** from the top and sides to simulate **sunlight** and **digital glow**.  
- **Glowing data streams** that emit a **cool blue light**, creating a **digital, data-driven ambiance**.

**Atmosphere:**  
- **Minimalistic and clean**, with **subtle particle effects** (e.g., digital dust, light trails) to suggest **movement and processing**.  
- **Muted, high-contrast colors** to maintain **professional clarity** and **focus**.

---

### **4. Materials & Textures**

**Gate Material:**  
- **Translucent, semi-organic material** with **subtle geometric patterns** (e.g., hexagonal or lattice structures) to suggest **structure and logic**.  
- **Surface texture** suggests **data flow** — a **smooth, glass-like surface** with **digital etchings** visible when light hits it.  
- **Glowing surface** when data is processed or decisions are made.

**Data Streams:**  
- **Digital ribbons** or **lines** made of **semi-transparent, glowing material**.  
- **Color variation** based on **data type** (e.g., red for risk, green for confidence, blue for neutral).  
- **Dynamic motion** to suggest **real-time processing**.

**Decision Nodes:**  
- **Small, glowing orbs** or **icons** that **emerge from the gate**.  
- **Material:** **glass-like with internal light** to suggest **transparency and validation**.  
- **Color:** **white with internal glow**, or **green for approved**, **red for rejected**, **yellow for pending**.

---

### **5. Color Palette**

**Primary Palette:**  
- **Cool tones** to reflect **calm precision** and **risk awareness**:  
  - **Blue** (trust, logic, transparency)  
  - **Gray** (professionalism, structure, control)  
  - **White** (clarity, purity, validation)  
- **Accent Colors:**  
  - **Green** (approved decisions)  
  - **Red** (rejected or high-risk)  
  - **Yellow** (pending or uncertain)

**Secondary Palette:**  
- **Subtle gradients** and **digital glow** to suggest **data flow** and **processing**.  
- **Minimal use of color** to maintain **professional clarity** and **focus**.

---

### **6. Visual Style & Tone**

**Visual Style:**  
- **Minimalist, high-contrast, clean** — reflecting the **evidence-first** and **transparent reasoning** brand principles.  
- **Digital and organic fusion** — the gate is both **physical and digital**, symbolizing the **intersection of human and machine** in trading.  
- **Subtle motion and glow** to suggest **real-time processing** and **dynamic decision-making**.

**Tone:**  
- **Calm, precise, and professional
